Image SEO works best when it becomes part of a repeatable publishing process.

## 1. Use clear filenames

Descriptive filenames make images easier to organize and can support better content management. Generic names like `IMG_0023.jpg` are weak compared to specific names that reflect the subject.

## 2. Resize images before publishing

The right dimensions reduce waste immediately. Images should match real layout needs instead of carrying oversized dimensions into production.

## 3. Compress every image

Compression should be standard, not optional. Even small savings add up across article archives, galleries, and landing pages.

## 4. Choose modern formats where practical

WebP and AVIF usually provide better efficiency than older defaults. They are often the right formats for performance-focused publishing.

## 5. Review the visual output

Do not publish based on file size alone. Always check that the result still looks sharp and trustworthy in the design.

## 6. Keep the workflow consistent

The best image SEO systems are simple enough to repeat every time. A clean tool and a clear checklist reduce mistakes and speed up publishing.
