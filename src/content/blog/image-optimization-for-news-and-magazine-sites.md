News and magazine sites publish often, which means small image decisions can multiply across thousands of pages.

## High-volume publishing needs consistency

A one-off optimization habit is not enough for editorial teams. The real win comes from building a repeatable process that works across authors, sections, and templates.

## Featured images matter most

Archive pages, category pages, and homepage blocks often surface the same images many times. Optimizing featured images improves more than just a single article.

## Balance speed and clarity

Editorial images must remain credible and readable. Readers still need clean visuals, especially when images contain people, products, events, or location details.

## Better format planning

WebP is often a dependable default for editorial publishing, while AVIF can reduce file size even further for selected assets. JPG still has a place where compatibility is the top priority.

## Best practice

Choose a workflow that every editor can follow quickly. Consistency is what keeps large article libraries performing well over time.
