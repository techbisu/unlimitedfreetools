Blogs usually need a mix of image formats rather than a single format everywhere.

## Featured images

For photographs and editorial thumbnails, WebP is usually the best default because it balances compression efficiency and compatibility.

## Screenshots and UI images

Screenshots with text and sharp edges often hold up better in PNG or high-quality WebP than in aggressive JPG compression.

## Transparent graphics

If transparency matters, PNG and WebP are usually the practical choices. AVIF can support transparency, but your workflow still needs to account for compatibility and review.

## Practical recommendation

Use WebP first for most blog imagery, keep PNG for lossless graphics and transparency-sensitive assets, and use JPG only when compatibility is the overriding concern.
