Blog images need to look clean without slowing the reading experience. That usually makes WebP the stronger default for modern publishing.

## Why JPG remains common

JPG has been used for years and still works well for many photographic images. It remains familiar and easy to export from almost any design workflow.

## Where WebP pulls ahead

WebP usually reaches smaller files at similar quality. For article images, banners, and thumbnails, that can make a meaningful difference across an entire site.

## When JPG is still useful

If a workflow, integration, or legacy publishing system depends on JPG, it can still be a practical format. Compatibility sometimes matters more than squeezing every possible byte.

## Best choice for many blogs

Use WebP for most new blog images when possible. Keep JPG available when the publishing stack requires it or when an editorial workflow depends on it.
