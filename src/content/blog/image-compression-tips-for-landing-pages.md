Landing pages often rely on a few important visuals, which makes each image choice more visible.

## Focus on the hero image first

The hero section is usually the most important image slot on the page. If that asset is too large, the entire page can feel delayed.

## Use realistic dimensions

Many landing page images are exported larger than necessary. Tightening dimensions to match the design can save a meaningful amount of file size before quality changes even begin.

## Avoid obvious artifacts

Compression should not distract from trust. If the image begins to look muddy or poorly produced, it can hurt the perceived quality of the offer.

## Choose the right format

WebP and AVIF are often strong choices for landing pages because they deliver efficient files with modern quality. The best format depends on the image style and the publishing workflow.

## Practical rule

Aim for the smallest file that still looks clean at first glance on both desktop and mobile. Landing page imagery should feel fast and confident, not over-processed.
