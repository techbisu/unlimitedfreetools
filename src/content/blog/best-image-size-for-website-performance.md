Large dimensions are one of the most common reasons websites ship bloated image files.

## Why oversized images slow pages down

An image exported at 4000 pixels wide may look no better than a 1400 pixel version in the actual layout. If the rendered space is smaller, the extra pixels only add file weight.

## Recommended sizes for common layouts

Hero images often work well between 1400 and 1920 pixels wide. Blog content images are usually comfortable between 900 and 1400 pixels wide. Product thumbnails can often stay much smaller depending on the layout density.

## Mobile-first matters

Many visitors arrive on smaller screens, so oversized desktop-first images can waste bandwidth. Planning around responsive image slots helps improve load time for both mobile and desktop users.

## Resize before heavy compression

If you want a smaller file, resizing usually gives a cleaner result than dropping quality too aggressively. A right-sized image with moderate compression often looks better than a huge image saved at low quality.

## Practical takeaway

Start with realistic dimensions for the layout, then compress and convert after resizing. That sequence almost always gives better performance than quality adjustments alone.
